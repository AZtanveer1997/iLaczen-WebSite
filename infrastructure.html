Infrastructure services

Infrastructure services refer to the basic and underlying systems, facilities, and equipment that support the functioning of a particular organization or community. These services can include, but are not limited to, transportation and communication networks, electrical and water systems, sewage and waste management, healthcare facilities, and emergency services. The main objective of infrastructure services is to provide a reliable and efficient platform for various activities and services to take place, enabling communities and organizations to thrive.

Data center management encompasses the daily responsibilities carried out by an organization to maintain a functioning data center. As data centers have become more sophisticated, the management of hardware, software, services, and physical infrastructure has become more intricate.

Server management

This involves overseeing the installation, maintenance, and repair of servers and related hardware components, such as storage devices, backup systems, and networking equipment.

Network management

This involves overseeing the operation and maintenance of data center networks, including local area networks (LANs), wide area networks (WANs), and the Internet, to ensure efficient data transmission and data security.

Cloud management

This involves managing cloud-based data center infrastructure, such as private, public, or hybrid cloud environments, to ensure efficient and secure data storage and processing.

Virtualization management

This involves managing virtualized environments, such as virtual servers and storage, to optimize data center resources and improve scalability.

Security management

This involves implementing and maintaining security measures, such as firewalls, encryption, and access controls, to protect data and prevent unauthorized access to the data center.

Backup and recovery management

This involves ensuring the protection and recovery of data stored in the data center, such as through regular backups and disaster recovery plans.

A monitoring service refers to a system or set of tools that helps to monitor various aspects of an IT infrastructure, including servers, applications, networks, and other elements. The goal of a monitoring service is to provide real-time insight into the performance, availability, and health of these components, and to alert administrators if any problems arise that could affect the smooth operation of the IT environment.
