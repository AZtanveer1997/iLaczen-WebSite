Risk management entails recognizing, evaluating, and arranging the potential hazards to an organization's information technology (IT) systems, data, and processes in order of priority. This process covers any potential threats or weaknesses that could compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or accessibility of the organization's IT assets. The objective of IT risk management is to reduce the impact of these risks and prevent or lessen their consequences by planning ahead and managing them proactively.

Risk identification

The first step in risk management is to identify the potential risks that an organization or individual may face. This can involve a review of past events, an assessment of current operations and processes.

Risk assessment

The next step is to assess the likelihood and potential impact of each risk. This can involve quantifying the likelihood of a risk occurring and the potential consequences if it does occur.

Risk prioritization

Based on the results of the risk assessment, risks can be prioritized in order of importance. This helps organizations focus their resources and efforts on the most significant risks.

Risk control

After risks have been identified and prioritized, strategies can be developed to manage or control those risks. This can include implementing new procedures, buying insurance, or investing in new technologies to reduce the likelihood or impact of a risk.
